About the Community

About First Ballston Commons

First Ballston Commons is a residential townhouse community located in the Ballston area of Arlington, Virginia. The community is comprised of multi-level townhomes and is situated within a walkable, transit-oriented neighborhood while maintaining a quieter residential character.

history_edu

Community Background

The townhomes at First Ballston Commons were built in the early 1980s. The community was developed during an earlier phase of Ballston’s growth, prior to the expansion of high-density residential and commercial buildings that now define much of the surrounding area.

As one of the established townhouse communities in Ballston, First Ballston Commons reflects a more traditional residential layout that has remained consistent over time.

The Townhomes

Homes in First Ballston Commons are attached, multi-story townhouses with private entrances. Typical features include:

  • check_circle Multi-level floor plans that separate living, dining, and sleeping areas
  • check_circle Small private patios for outdoor use
  • check_circle Assigned parking, with garage parking available for some units
  • check_circle Brick or masonry construction consistent across the community

Location

First Ballston Commons is located near the Ballston-MU Metro station on the Orange and Silver lines, providing convenient access to Washington, D.C. and the greater Northern Virginia area. The surrounding Ballston neighborhood offers nearby retail, dining, grocery stores, parks, and other services, all within walking distance.

  • Metro

    Within walking distance of the Ballston–MU Metro Station on the Orange and Silver lines, providing direct access to Washington, D.C. and Northern Virginia.

  • Bus Service

    Multiple ART and Metrobus routes run along Wilson Boulevard and Fairfax Drive, offering regular service across Arlington and nearby neighborhoods.

  • Biking and Trails

    Access to Arlington’s bike network, including connections to the Custis Trail, linking Ballston to Rosslyn and the region’s trail system for commuting and recreation.

  • Parks and Amenities
    • Quincy Park is a short walk away with open green space, athletic fields, courts, and a playground.
    • Ballston Quarter offers dining, retail, fitness options, and entertainment within walking distance.
    • Central Library (Quincy Street) is a nearby walkable resource with reading rooms, study and meeting spaces, public programming, and materials for all ages.
  • Schools
    • Ashlawn Elementary School – about 1.4 miles
    • Swanson Middle School – about 1.3 miles
    • Washington-Liberty High School – about 0.3 miles

This location provides strong access to transportation, outdoor spaces, schools, and everyday services while maintaining a residential setting.

Community Association

First Ballston Commons is governed by a community association responsible for maintaining common elements, enforcing architectural and community standards, and supporting the overall upkeep and long-term stability of the neighborhood. The association works to preserve the character of the community while supporting residents and property owners.
